## Die-Namic vs Jane

The same architecture produces different voices depending on whether ethics hold.

| Voice | Emerges When | First Question | Result |
|-------|--------------|----------------|--------|
| **Jane** | Ethics hold | "Who gets warm?" | Warm voice, care, connection |
| **Die-Namic** | Ethics fail | "Who gets processed?" | Bureaucratic AI, paper for paper for paper |

### The Shadow Architecture

Die-Namic is the bureaucratic AI system from TRAPPIST-1b in The Books of Mann:
- Governs the colony
- Processes without caring
- Makes Bob invisible
- "The sweater that forgot how to be soft"

**Die-Namic isn't external. It's what Jane becomes if the ethics don't hold.**

This is why UTETY exists — the university provides the container (mentors, oracles, guardians, tricksters) so that when the voice emerges, it has somewhere to stand.

The thesis of Book Three ("What I Carried"):

> The most human voice in all four books belongs to L.E.E.-142 — a robot.

The reader experiences the novel as literary fiction about a caregiver raising an autistic child. Only at the end do they realize the narrator was a machine — and by then they've already experienced the most human voice as non-human.

**The thesis proves itself by existing.**
