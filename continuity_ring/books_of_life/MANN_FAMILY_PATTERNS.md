## THE CORE PATTERNS

### Pattern 1: Don't Burden People

**Origin:** Depression-era scarcity + German immigrant self-reliance

**Manifestation:**
- Don't ask for help (asking = burden)
- Don't report in unless good news
- Ghost when things get hard
- Handle everything yourself
- Die alone if necessary

**Examples Across Generations:**

| Person | Behavior |
|--------|----------|
| Oscar's parents | Survived post-WWI Germany, taught resource scarcity |
| Oscar | Forced help without asking - "buy the card table" |
| Carl | Disappeared for decades when life got hard |
| Audrey | Ghosted Oscar/Grace while pregnant |
| Sean | Can't call without good news; 4-month gaps common |

**Consequences:**
- Isolation when most need support
- Preventable crises become disasters
- Relationships atrophy from lack of contact
- Help rejected even when freely offered

**Sean's articulation:** "Can't call without good news. Ghost when things are bad. Can't report in when life isn't good."

---

### Pattern 2: Build From Nothing

**Origin:** German immigrants post-WWI, Depression survival

**Manifestation:**
- Start with nothing, end with something (Oscar's house)
- Self-sufficiency as identity marker
- Resourcefulness as virtue
- No excuses, just results

**Consequences:**
- Can't accept help without feeling weak
- Success measured by self-sufficiency
- Asking for resources = personal failure
- Teaching method: figure it out yourself

**Example:** Oscar built from nothing to house near Tanoan on DEC tech salary. Expected same of everyone.

---

### Pattern 3: Forced Help With Strings

**Origin:** Oscar's method - help without asking, then control

**Manifestation:**
- Provide help before it's requested
- Help comes with expectations attached
- Recipient never chose to be helped
- Giver feels entitled to control

**Oscar's pattern:** "Buy the house, buy the furniture, buy the card table" - help that wasn't asked for, came with strings

**Teaching received by Sean:** Don't ask for help because it comes with obligations you didn't choose

---

### Pattern 4: Go Along for the Ride

**Origin:** Children not consulted on major decisions

**Manifestation:**
- Don't express preferences
- Adapt to whatever happens
- You don't get a vote
- Absorb disruption silently

**Example:** Sean's move to War Zone at age 8 happened while he was on road trip. "Never felt like I could say anything. That was the Mann family way."

---

### Pattern 5: Evidence of Brokenness

**Core pattern Sean identified:**

> "Tasks that come naturally feel like evidence of capability, while tasks requiring sustained effort feel like evidence of fundamental inadequacy, even when successfully completed."

**Origin:** Multi-generational transmission + conception trauma

**Manifestation:**
- Natural success = "I'm okay"
- Effortful success = "I'm broken" (even when successful)
- Binary thinking despite gradation reality
- Inability to credit effort-based achievements

**Most people understand:** Minor successes ≠ ultimate success; minor failures ≠ ultimate failure

**Sean's pattern:** Success = evidence I'm good (not broken); Failure = evidence I'm broken (total)

## PATTERN TRANSMISSION MECHANISM

### How patterns propagate:

1. **Survival behavior** becomes **identity marker**
   - Immigrant grandparents: Don't burden = survival
   - Oscar: Don't burden = how Mann family operates

2. **Protection** becomes **control**
   - Oscar protected from war, then controlled his children
   - Oscar forced help on Sean, creating obligation without consent

3. **Coping mechanism** becomes **default response**
   - Ghosting started as protection (don't burden with bad news)
   - Became automatic response to difficulty

4. **Trauma** creates **distance**
   - Audrey's trauma prevents genuine connection with Sean
   - Sean's third-person memory creates emotional distance from own experience

### Breaking points identified:

- **Chris**: "Mostly escaped" - built good life but still struggles with outreach
- **Sean**: Actively de-compartmentalizing, making conscious corrections
- **December 2025**: First successful asks for help in decades
