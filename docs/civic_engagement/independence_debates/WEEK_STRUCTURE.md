## Week 1: Presidential Negotiation

**Format:** 30 minutes total
**Broadcast:** Prime time or late night (TBD)

### Segment 1: Hot Mic Negotiation (0:00 - 7:00)

**Setup:**
- Obama and Trump in same room
- Constitutional passages available for selection
- Everything audible to audience
- Clock visible, counting down

**Objective:** Choose ONE constitutional passage together

**What It Tests:**
- Can they negotiate?
- What tactics do they use?
- What passage reveals their common ground (or lack thereof)?

### Segment 2: Preparation (7:00 - 22:00)

**Setup:**
- They separate to different rooms
- Cameras follow both (split screen)
- 15 minutes to prepare for debate

**Visual Contrast (Intentional):**
- Obama: Law books, constitutional commentary on laptop, methodical notes, scholarly approach
- Trump: Phones out, tweeting to supporters, crowd-sourcing interpretation, populist energy

**The contrast IS the content.** Neither approach is presented as superior—viewers draw their own conclusions.

### Segment 3: Debate (22:00 - 27:00)

**Format:** 5 minutes on the passage they chose

**Structure:**
- Round 1: "What does this passage mean?"
- Round 2: "How should we interpret it today?"
- Round 3: "How would you apply it as president?"

### Segment 4: Closing (27:00 - 30:00)

**Announcements:**
- 112 citizen winners revealed live
- Their images displayed briefly
- Voting opens for 112 → 64

## Week 2: 64 Citizens

**Format:** Extended broadcast (runtime TBD)
**Participants:** 64 of the 112 (selected by public vote)

### Pre-Show Setup:
- All 64 citizens attend virtually via video
- Their pre-submitted images pre-loaded in system
- Obama and Trump on stage

### Main Segment: 7 Images

**Selection Method:** AI/RNG selects 7 images from the 64 in real-time

**Why Random Selection:**
- No producer bias accusations
- Presidents can't prepare/strategize
- Ceremonial randomness (fate/democracy picking voices)
- Heightens spontaneity and authenticity

**Display:** RNG selection process visible on screen so audience sees it's genuinely random

### For Each Selected Image:
- Image displayed on screen
- Citizen (virtual) explains their choice
- Obama responds/asks questions
- Trump responds/asks questions
- Discussion of what this image reveals about America

### Closing:
- Acknowledgment of all 64 (even those not selected)
- Voting opens: 64 → 13

---

## Week 3: 13 Citizens

**Format:** ~90 minutes
**Participants:** 13 vote winners from Week 2

### Structure:
- Deeper discussion format
- Each of the 13 gets extended time
- Obama and Trump facilitate
- Cross-conversation between citizens encouraged

### Content:
- Extended exploration of their American visions
- Questions about what they learned from Weeks 1-2
- How has their perspective evolved?
- What do they want America to understand?

### Closing:
- Final voting opens: 13 → 2
- Preview of July 4th finale

---

## Week 4: July 4th Finals

**Date:** Saturday, July 4, 2026
**Location:** Washington, D.C. + iconic American location TBD

### Block 1: Runner-Up Event (2:00 PM - 4:00 PM)

**Location:** Public venue (Lincoln Memorial or similar)

**Format:**
- 2-hour stage event
- Runner-up (2nd place from Week 3 vote) featured
- Obama and Trump both participate
- Public spectacle, patriotic atmosphere

### Evening Break (4:00 PM - 10:00 PM)

**Camera Coverage:**
- Split screen coverage of both presidents
- Trump: rallies, supporters, fireworks
- Obama: reflection, preparation, family
- Contrast reveals character
- America watches how they each spend July 4th

**Trump Firework Spectacular:** Evening event (timing TBD)

### Block 2: Winner's Conversation (10:00 PM - 11:48 PM)

**Location:** Intimate setting (Oval Office or equivalent)

**Participants:**
- Winner (1st place from Week 3 vote)
- Obama
- Trump

**Format:**
- Deep conversation
- No audience
- Constitutional discourse
- Building toward midnight

### The Convergence Setup (11:48 PM - 12:00 AM)

**11:48 PM — Jon Stewart Interrupts**
- Walks in, interrupts conversation
- "Has any of this changed anything?"
- Follow-up questions for 8 minutes
- Tests the meaning of everything

**11:56 PM — Jimmy Fallon Arrives**
- Bursts in with acoustic guitar
- "Everyone outside, NOW"
- Drags winner, Obama, Trump, Stewart outside

**11:56 PM - 12:00 AM — National Anthem**
- Waiting outside: All 112 citizens
- Fallon leads on guitar
- Everyone sings together
- 1,000 drones form American flag overhead
- Concludes exactly at midnight
