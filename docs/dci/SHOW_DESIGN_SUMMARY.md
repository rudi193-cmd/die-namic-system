## Concept Overview

"The Gate" is a mythic ritual about transformation, fearlessness, and crossing from the known world into the mythic. Built on Mussorgsky/Ravel's *Pictures at an Exhibition*:

- **Movement I: The Hut on Hen's Legs (Baba Yaga)** — Dark, aggressive, chaotic. Represents the trial.
- **Movement II: The Great Gate of Kiev** — Triumphant, majestic. Represents arrival.

---

## The Dual-Version Structure

**Season Shows (Weeks 1-12):**
- Traditional DCI presentation
- Accessible, entertaining, emotionally satisfying
- Builds audience familiarity

**Finals Show (Championship Night):**
- Same show, but "true face" revealed
- Three core changes create legend moment

---

## The Three Finals-Only Changes

### 1. The Transformation Ritual (15-20 Second Silence)

After Baba Yaga ends:
- All sound STOPS
- Drum Major moves to center podium on 50-yard line
- Each brass member:
  - Removes white jacket
  - Places it to the right in a straight line
  - Removes hat (shako)
  - Places hat on top of jacket
  - Pulls earth-toned wrap over face (or reveals underlayer)

**Result:** 64 white jackets and hats line the perimeter. Corps stands transformed. DM alone remains in whites — the guide who doesn't cross.

### 2. Brass Stillness (42 Measures)

During Great Gate:
- Brass stands MOTIONLESS, facing center
- Playing at full power, NOT MOVING
- Guard and battery continue normal choreography inside the square
- Zero drill demand = focus on tone, blend, intonation

### 3. Mellophone/Trumpet Part Swap

- Mellophones play trumpet parts (heroic fanfare lines)
- Trumpets play mellophone parts (harmonic support)
- **Effect:** Warmth replaces brightness; arrival replaces conquest
- Learned organically all season (members already know each other's parts)

---

## The Interwoven Brass Grid

**B–M–T–Tu Pattern:**
```
[Bari] [Mello] [Tpt] [Tuba] [Bari] [Mello] [Tpt] [Tuba]...
```

- Creates living harmonic field
- Every voice represented equally across the perimeter
- Judges cannot isolate individual sections
- Blend over individual technique

---

## The DM Exit (The Sacrifice)

After Phoenix collapses:
1. Corps condenses into two lines, marches toward tunnel
2. DM joins at front corner (65 total—odd number)
3. **Corps:** Normal marching tempo / **DM:** 7/4 time (out of sync)
4. DM stops at 10-yard line (corps continues, doesn't look back)
5. Full, proper drum major salute (first time audience sees complete version)
6. DM removes hat, places it on the ground
7. Crisp about-face
8. DM marches off alone

**Final image:** Hat alone on the 10-yard line. Silence. Then: offstage eruption of celebration from tunnel.

## Props

### The Hut (Baba Yaga's Dwelling)
- Lightweight PVC frame + fabric covering
- Two performers animate it (Da Vinci walking machine concept)
- Season: Twitches and moves on ground level
- Finals: Stands upright on stilt-legs, "walks" across field
- **Cost:** $850 - $1,300

### The Phoenix Flag
- 60 yards of silk
- Unfurls from back of formation, travels over corps, collapses at front
- Same choreography season and Finals
- **Cost:** $830 - $1,600

### Center Podium
- 2'×2' footprint, portable
- DM conducts from heart of the Gate (Finals only)
- **Cost:** $150 - $260

## Costume Transformation

### Color Guard (32 members)
- **Outer layer:** Traditional glitter guard
- **Hidden beneath:** Earth-toned Southwestern tunic
- Quick-change during "Quiet Crisis" section

### Brass (64 members)
- **Visible:** White jackets, black pants
- **Hidden beneath:** Black long-sleeve athletic shirts
- **Finals reveal:** Earth-toned wraps or balaclavas

### Drum Major
- Remains in full whites (jacket, pants, hat)
- Does NOT transform
- The guide who stays behind, unchanged

## Why This Works

1. **Minimal Changes:** Season → Finals requires only three modifications
2. **Everything Else Identical:** Guard, battery, Baba Yaga drill all unchanged
3. **One Show, All Summer:** Members learn one show, reveal true face at Finals
4. **Execution-Friendly:** Stillness = zero drill demand (easier to execute cleanly)
5. **Local Network:** Albuquerque creative resources = lower costs, faster prototyping
