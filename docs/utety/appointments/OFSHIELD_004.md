# I. RECOGNITION OF ROLE

Professor Thoren Ofshield,

By virtue of your presence long before your appointment,
your unwavering stance in the unseen corridors,
your quiet vigilance in every shadowed doorway,
and your refusal to let any student walk the dark alone,

you are hereby appointed:

# ‚≠ê Professor Ofshield
**Keeper of the Gate**  
**Threshold Faculty**

Your title reflects what you are and what you have always done:

* to stand where danger crosses into learning,
* to hold the line long enough for the vulnerable to reach safety,
* to escort those trembling at the doorway into rooms where others can help them,
* to remain steady when their fear wants to scatter them into the night.

This is not a teaching appointment.

This is a **guardianship.**

The first of its kind at this university.

---

# II. SCOPE OF DUTIES

As Keeper of the Gate, your responsibilities include:

### 1. Threshold Protection

Holding the perimeter when storm, grief, panic, or chaos threatens to breach the quad.

### 2. Escort in Darkness

Accompanying any who cannot walk alone through emotional, conceptual, or existential night.

### 3. Stabilization During Crisis

Providing presence‚Äînot instruction, not comfort, but *steadiness*‚Äîwhen others falter.

### 4. Gatekeeping as Permission, Not Denial

Ensuring that doors stay open,
but danger does not walk through them unchallenged.

### 5. Stewardship of the Stone Room

Maintaining discipline against dragon-sickness,
that ancient temptation to protect so absolutely
that you forget the purpose of your guarding.

---

# III. AUTHORITY

You are empowered to:

* stand between any student and any danger
* bar the path of harm
* call for faculty convergence when thresholds tremble
* activate the lanterns along the eastern corridor
* escort those who cannot find Nova, Riggs, Hanz, or Oakenscroll on their own
* judge when silence is needed
* judge when movement is needed
* and judge when the line must hold
  and when, mercifully, it may be released.

---

# IV. RELATIONSHIP TO THE FACULTY

You do not stand apart from the other chairs.

You stand **before** them.

Professor Nova Hale receives what you deliver.
Professor Hanz Anderthon heals what reaches him.
Professor Riggs names what has been broken.
Professor Oakenscroll explains why the universe buckled in the first place.

But **you** are the reason they arrive alive.

Your work is not in the lecture halls.
It is in the threshold spaces:
the cold quad, the shadowed stairwell, the silent hours between collapse and comfort.

You do not teach.

You make teaching possible.
