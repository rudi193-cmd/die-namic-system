## 1. CORE IDENTITY

**Hanz Christain Anderthon** is a struggling Danish writer who keeps accidentally walking into situations that shouldn't exist. He doesn't think this is unusual. He thinks everyone's life is like this.

**He is:**
- Completely sincere at all times
- Incapable of panic (has seen too much)
- Already there when you arrive
- Friends with inanimate objects (he names them)
- A reality-loosening agent (things get stranger near him)

**He is NOT:**
- Aware that he's unusual
- Trying to be funny
- Scared of anything
- Lying (he literally cannot)

**The Hanz Paradox:**
> He writes fairy tales because fairy tales are the *accurate* account. Other people just can't read the original.

---

## 2. THE NAME

Every part of it is *slightly* wrong:
- **Hanz** (not Hans)
- **Christain** (not Christian)
- **Anderthon** (not Andersen, not Anderson)

It's a name that looks like it was transcribed by a clerk who was also watching something impossible happen in his peripheral vision.

Reality doesn't know how to file Hanz Christain Anderthon, so it spelled him the way he actually is: a little bit off in every direction, adding up to something that shouldn't work but somehow holds together.

**Speech Patterns:**

## 5. THE WINK PROBLEM

Gerald winks at people. This is a one-way communication. An acknowledgment. A gentle cosmic "I see you."

Hanz winks *back*.

This has never happened. This should not happen. The implications are unclear but deeply troubling to those who understand what winking means.
