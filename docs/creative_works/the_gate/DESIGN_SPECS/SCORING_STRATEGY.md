## Overview

"The Gate" is designed to exploit the DCI judging system. The interleaved brass grid and stillness create chaos for individual caption judges while maximizing ensemble and effect scores.

**Net result:** You win the trade under the current scoring model.

---

## The Judge Experience

### Marching Judge (Visual Individual)

**Season Shows:**
- Normal drill, normal assessment
- Following sections, watching footwork, evaluating spacing

**Finals (Great Gate, Stillness):**

> "Okay, brass just... stopped. They're standing still. For 42 measures. What do I... judge? Their posture? Their... standing?"

The marching judge has **nothing to do** for 42 measures. The brass isn't marching. There's no footwork to critique, no spacing to evaluate, no transitions to assess.

**She's left with nothing to do, and that ABSENCE becomes the most present thing on the field.**

### Individual Brass Caption Judges (Trumpet, Mellophone, Baritone, Tuba)

**Season Shows:**
- Each judge follows their assigned section
- Can hear their section clearly (grouped together)
- Standard assessment: intonation, balance, technique

**Finals (Interleaved Grid):**

The 8×8 formation uses B-M-T-Tu interleaving. Sections are scattered.

**Trumpet Judge:**
> "Where are the trumpets? One here, one there, one over there. If I stand near THIS trumpet, I can't hear THAT trumpet. If I move to the center, I hear... everyone. All the brass. But I can't isolate just the trumpets."

**Mellophone Judge:**
> "I'm supposed to judge the mellophone SECTION. But there IS no section—they're woven into a grid. Do I judge the mello PART (which I can hear)? Or do I try to track down individual mello players?"

**All Individual Brass Judges:**
They cluster around the DM podium (center of the square) because that's the only place they can hear the full ensemble. They're tripping over each other, cramming into a tiny space, trying to do their jobs.

**The paradox:** The most CHAOTIC judging experience surrounding the most DISCIPLINED performance.

### Brass Ensemble Judge

**Season Shows:**
- Standard assessment of blend, balance, intonation

**Finals (Interleaved Grid):**

> "This is the most BALANCED brass sound I've heard all season. Every voice is present, no one dominates, it's a CHOIR."

The interwoven grid creates unprecedented blend. No section is louder or spatially isolated. It's a **unified brass sound** like nothing else.

### Music Effect Judge

**Season Shows:**
- Standard assessment of musical impact

**Finals:**

> "The interwoven grid created a SONIC WALL. It was unforgettable. The sound is omnidirectional, immersive, overwhelming."

The grid isn't just a visual choice—it's **sonic architecture**. Every yard line has all four voices.

### General Effect Judges

**Finals:**

> "Bold. Effective. Risk + execution = reward."

The transformation ritual, the stillness, the interleaved grid, the DM exit—all are **bold design choices**. GE rewards intention and impact.

## The Scoring Trade-Off

### You LOSE Points In:

**Individual Brass Captions (Trumpet, Mellophone, Baritone, Tuba)**
- Judges can't isolate sections effectively
- Harder to give detailed feedback
- **Estimated loss:** 0.3-0.8 points per caption = **~1.5-2.5 points total**

### You GAIN Points In:

**Brass Ensemble**
- Unprecedented blend
- Balance across all voices is perfect (by design)
- **Estimated gain:** +1 to 2 points

**Music Effect**
- Sonic impact of the interwoven grid is massive
- Creates a unique, memorable sound
- **Estimated gain:** +1 to 1.5 points

**General Effect (Music)**
- Bold design choice recognized
- Risk + execution = reward
- **Estimated gain:** +1 to 2 points

**Visual Effect**
- Stillness creates powerful contrast
- Transformation ritual is theatrical
- **Estimated gain:** +0.5 to 1 point

### Net Result:

**Loss:** ~1.5-2.5 points (individual brass captions)
**Gain:** ~3.5-6.5 points (ensemble, effect, GE)

**NET:** **+2 to 4 points** — You WIN the trade.

## Why This Works Under Current Scoring

### Old Scoring Model (Pre-2010s):
- Individual captions mattered MORE
- Judges needed to hear sections clearly
- Sectional balance was king

### Current Scoring Model (2010s-Present):
- **Effect captions carry more weight** (GE is nearly 40% of total score)
- **Ensemble matters more than individual sections**
- **Bold design choices are rewarded** if they serve the show

The interleaved grid:
- Hurts individual captions (old model priority)
- HELPS ensemble + effect (new model priority)
- **Result: You win under the current system**

## The Mellophone/Trumpet Swap

### Additional Scoring Advantage

At Finals, mellophones play trumpet parts and trumpets play mellophone parts.

**What This Does:**
- Demonstrates **section versatility**—both sections can handle different voicings
- Shows **arrangement depth**—same music works beautifully with inverted parts
- **Risk factor**—swapping parts at Finals is bold; executing it cleanly scores

**How It's Learned:**
- Members absorb each other's parts organically all season
- Singing on the bus, noodling in tunnels, listening 50 times a day
- By Finals, both sections already know both parts
- No extra rehearsal needed—just cleaning the balance

## The DM Exit

### Why 7/4?

The drum major marches off in 7/4 time while the corps marches in standard tempo.

**Visual Effect:**
- DM is out of sync with everyone else
- Moving slower, deliberate, isolated
- The corps continues; the DM stops at the 10-yard line

**Emotional Effect:**
- The salute is the "perfected" version—first time audience sees it complete
- The hat placement is a sacrifice
- The solo 7/4 march is a crossing they make alone

**GE Impact:** Maximum. This is the moment that makes the show legendary.
