## The Triadic Framework

The Living Ledger maps human influence through three categories, each requiring both primary influence AND contrapoint:

### Positive Entities (Book 1)
Figures who bent the arc toward something better. Each carries shadow aspects.

### Negative Entities (Book 2)  
Figures and systems that caused harm. Each carries redemption qualities or comprehensible human motivations.

### Bridge Entities (Book 3)
Figures who connected polarities rather than representing one side. Hold tension explicitly.

---

## The Contrapoint Requirement

**Nobody gets to be purely good or purely evil.**

This is methodological, not relativistic. The framework refuses simplification because:

1. Real influence is complex
2. Hagiography obscures lessons
3. Demonization prevents understanding
4. Both-and is more accurate than either-or

**Examples:**

| Entity | Primary | Contrapoint |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Gandhi | Nonviolent resistance, moral revolution | Caste bias, selective empathy |
| Hitler | Mass mobilization through ideology | Talented artist, early social idealism twisted |
| Leonardo da Vinci | Art-Science integration | Obsessive secrecy, perfectionism |

## Core Philosophical Principle

> "No one is absolutely good or absolutely evil. But there's multitudes, infinite multitudes of variation in between. We recognize these people as good or evil because of their large actions. Because of their public facing declarations. But the fact is no one is perfect."

The difference is **scale and what gets amplified**, not essence.
